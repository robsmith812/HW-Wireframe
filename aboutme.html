Hello & Welcome

A Little About Me...

Born and raised in Sacramento, CA, I spent my childhood in different areas including: Meadowview and Greenhaven, with a mojority of my youth spent in South Sacramento. At the age of 17, I moved to San Francisco to attend University of San Francisco for undergrad. After obtaining my Bachelors Degree, I moved to San Leandro for around 3 years for work. I then got a job in Southern California which had me move to San Diego, my favoirte city that I have lived in. After 2 years, I moved back to the Bay Area to live in Santa Clara while working on my Master's Degree at University of San Francisco. Shortly after completeing the 2 year program, I got a job at Sonoma State University, where I am still currently employed as a Campus Life Advisor in Student Involvement. I have been living in the North Bay for 5 years now, with my next journey being gaining a knowledge base in web development.

Hobbies

Soccer

The sport of soccer was truly my first love. I started playing at the age of 5 with my dad being my coach. I quickly moved on to playing on competitive youth teams which had my family and I traveling almost every weekend. I also had the opportunity to tryout and play for Northern California State Team, which lead me to a tryout for the Western United States team, that I did not make. However, these experiences developed me enough to be a member of the NCAA Division I Men's Soccer Team at University of San Francisco. I still enjoy playing and coaching to this day.

Gamer

I was always faced with the decision to practice my craft of soccer or put in a couple more hours in the game I was currently playing. Video games have given me a community and brought me to meet my closest friends. Overtime, I have come to believe that games offer much more than satisfaction for completing challenges, but they truly offer a set a transferrable skills that can be applied in serveral different settings. Further, when disected, they offer deep commentary on society touching on topics like: race, gender roles, sexual orientation, and socio-economic status. Some of my favorite series include: Final Fantasy, Fallout, Bioshock, and Dark Souls.
